2. Storage Instructions

  • Store at room temperature (25–30°C).
  • Protect from light and moisture.
  • Keep out of reach of children.

3. Chemistry of Drug

Chemical Class: Hormones

4. Pharmacokinetics

Absorption50%–80% orally; may decrease with age.
Distribution99% protein-bound (TBG, TBPA, TBA); Vd: 8.7–9.7 L/kg; does not cross placental barrier.
MetabolismSequential deiodination in liver; conjugated with glucuronides/sulfates; enterohepatic circulation.
EliminationRenal and fecal; 20%–70% excreted in stool; urinary excretion decreases with age.
Half-lifeApproximately 6–7 days.

5. Clinical Pharmacology

Pharmacological Class: Hormones

Mechanism of Action (MOA) Levothyroxine regulates gene transcription and protein synthesis by binding thyroid hormone receptors in the nucleus (T3/T4), initiating mRNA synthesis.
Contraindications
  • Uncorrected adrenal insufficiency
  • Untreated thyrotoxicosis
  • Acute myocardial infarction
  • Hypersensitivity
Precautions
  • Use cautiously in cardiovascular disease.
  • Monitor closely during pregnancy and lactation.

6. Indications

  • Treatment of hypothyroidism
  • Suppression of TSH in thyroid cancer and nodules

7. Dose Adjustment

Required during pregnancy or in elderly patients with comorbidities.

8. Adverse Drug Reactions (ADRs)

GeneralFatigue, increased appetite, weight loss
CNSHeadache, nervousness, anxiety
MusculoskeletalTremors, muscle spasms
RespiratoryDyspnea
GITDiarrhea, abdominal cramps, vomiting

9. Drug-Drug Interactions

Reduced T4 Absorption With:

  • Phosphate binders
  • Orlistat
  • Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs)
  • Bile acid sequestrants

Altered Metabolism With:

  • Phenobarbital
  • Rifampin

10. Drug-Food Interactions

  • Soy, walnuts, cottonseed meal, and high-fiber diets reduce absorption.
  • Grapefruit juice lowers bioavailability.
